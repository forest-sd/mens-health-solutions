Nutrition Tips for Men

Eat to support your hormones, energy, and performance.

Nutrition Fundamentals for Men

What you eat directly affects your testosterone, energy, body composition, and how you feel every day. Here are the key principles that actually matter.

Protein: The Non-Negotiable

Men need adequate protein to maintain muscle mass, support hormone production, and stay satiated. Aim for 0.7-1g per pound of body weight daily. Best sources: lean meats, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, whey protein.

Fats: Don't Fear Them

Testosterone is literally made from cholesterol. Extremely low-fat diets tank testosterone. Include healthy fats daily: olive oil, avocados, nuts, fatty fish, eggs. Aim for 25-35% of calories from fat.

Foods That Support Testosterone

  • Eggs: Complete protein + cholesterol + vitamin D
  • Oysters/shellfish: Highest dietary source of zinc (critical for testosterone)
  • Fatty fish: Omega-3s reduce inflammation and support hormone production
  • Cruciferous vegetables: Broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts help metabolize excess estrogen
  • Garlic: Contains allicin, shown to reduce cortisol and support testosterone
  • Pomegranate: Shown to increase testosterone levels and improve blood flow

Foods to Limit

  • Excessive alcohol: Directly suppresses testosterone production
  • Processed foods: High in inflammatory oils, sugar, and endocrine disruptors
  • Soy (excessive): Phytoestrogens may affect hormone balance in high amounts
  • Sugar: Spikes insulin, promotes fat storage, reduces testosterone

Practical Tips

  • Eat protein at every meal
  • Don't skip meals (cortisol spikes from fasting stress)
  • Hydrate adequately (dehydration impairs everything)
  • Eat whole foods 80% of the time — don't obsess over perfection
  • Consider supplementing vitamin D, magnesium, and zinc if bloodwork shows deficiency
