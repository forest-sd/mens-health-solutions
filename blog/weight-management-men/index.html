Weight Management for Men Over 35

Why it gets harder — and what actually works.

Why Weight Loss Gets Harder

If you've noticed the scale creeping up despite not eating more or exercising less, you're not imagining things. Several factors converge after 35:

  • Declining testosterone: Less testosterone means less muscle mass, slower metabolism, and easier fat storage — especially around the midsection
  • Insulin resistance: Cells become less responsive to insulin, making your body more likely to store calories as fat
  • Decreased growth hormone: GH drops significantly, reducing your body's ability to burn fat and build muscle
  • Thyroid changes: Even subclinical thyroid slowdown impacts metabolism
  • Cortisol: Chronic stress elevates cortisol, which directly promotes abdominal fat storage
  • Sleep quality: Poor sleep disrupts hunger hormones (ghrelin and leptin) and tanks testosterone

What Doesn't Work

  • Crash diets (destroy muscle and metabolism)
  • Cardio-only approaches (without resistance training)
  • Ignoring hormones (the biggest overlooked factor)
  • Willpower alone (biology beats willpower every time)

What Actually Works

  • Hormone optimization: Fix the foundation. If testosterone, thyroid, or insulin are off, everything else is an uphill battle
  • Resistance training: Builds muscle, boosts metabolism, improves insulin sensitivity, and increases testosterone
  • Protein priority: Higher protein intake preserves muscle during fat loss and improves satiety
  • Sleep optimization: 7-8 hours of quality sleep is non-negotiable for hormonal health
  • Stress management: Lowering cortisol enables fat loss
  • Medical guidance: A personalized plan based on your actual bloodwork and metabolic profile
